Love songs

The next songs I want to share are in a totally different mood: exit burlesque, enter romance and poetry, with La Non Demande en Mariage and Les Passantes. Let me start with a general word about Brassens' romantic love songs. They show how wrong the general perception is about Brassens' melodies, often assumed to be "basic", "secondary to lyrics" and "all alike". Just try singing any of his songs with some accuracy! (For the record, his guitar parts frighten even pro guitarists because he never follows standard chord patterns…). Brassens does have a very specific musical style, with strong harmonic and rhythmical features that make his songs sound alike to newcomers. Just as you may think that all Chinese people look alike! But if you dig in, you find how different (and often complex) they are.

The melodies of his romance songs illustrate this point because they also have more conspicuous differences when compared with his other songs: they are more "obviously" romantic, beautifully underlined by a second guitar and a double bass. But they keep Brassens' trademark: their capacity to maximise the impact of lyrics. That flow, that mood, and those "hanging in the air" moments where a note keeps you waiting for the next word… irresistible.

So… La Non Demande en Mariage is a super romantic love letter to Brassens' life-long lover and muse Joha Heiman, aka Püppchen. As the song suggests, they never married nor lived under the same roof – although their relationship remained very strong and faithful until his death. Brassens wrote several songs for Püppchen. La Non Demande en Mariage is my favourite, on par with the wonderful Saturne – the first two verses of which I will add as a bonus…
